WHAT EXACTLY IS ANIME ?

Anime is a cultural and entertainment phenomenon that enjoys excellent popularity internationally. It is an art related to manga (Japanese comics), cosplay (the use of costumes), and other disciplines and trends.







HISTORY OF MANGA

The manga origin as a way of telling stories and definitions of the word began during the second half of the nineteenth century and the beginning of the twentieth century, 1868-1912, during a period of cultural and economic opening under the mandate of Emperor Mutsuhito.

The word manga (漫画) is composed of two kanji; man (informal) and ga (drawing). It translates to whimsical drawings or doodles. This term was coined to define the art that emerged from the union of the graphic style of traditional Japanese painting and Western-style comics.

Currently, the word manga is used in Japan to refer to "comics" in a general way. However, outside of Japan, this word is used more specifically to define the Japanese style of drawing and storytelling.



MANGA IN THE 20TH CENTURY

The cartoonist Rakuten Kitazawa, was the author of a comic called Tagosaku to Mokube no Tokyo Kenbutsu. His work (1902) is officially considered the first manga in history. It's The story of two peasants who takes a vacation to Tokyo. Although they are rural characters with little knowledge of modern life and the big city, when they arrive, they behave in a silly and rude way.









ORIGIN OF ANIME

The animation film producer Toei was one of the first to stand out. The first production was released in May 1957 and was titled Koneko no rakugaki (Kitten Graffiti). It was a 13-minute short film.




At this time, we must highlight the significant influence of Osamu Tezuka, considered by many as "the god of manga and anime." He was a critical, creative piece for the industry, remarkably driving and shaping many characteristics that define anime today. In addition, he was the creator of the legendary Tetsuwan Atomu series, known as Astroboy.

IMPORTANCE OF MANGA AND ANIME FOR THE JAPANESE ECONOMY

Japan is a country where people read a lot and not just manga. So the number of existing bookstores in Japanese cities is striking.

Manga alone represents more than 23% of the entire Japanese publishing industry. In total, 40% of everything that is published in the country is manga, an average of 11,000 manga are published a year,



Recently, tourism in Japan has increased, boosting its monumental landscape, technological, and cultural heritage, and much of the blame lies with manga and anime. Many tourists who visit Japan tend to be influenced by Japanese audiovisual culture, with their minds set on childhood stories and fantastic stories.
